- **Research tool, not a sales tool.** You can find companies, but you can't build outreach sequences, track engagement, or manage pipeline from Crunchbase.

## Which One Should You Choose?

**If you're a solo founder or small team selling to funded startups:**
Start with FundedList (for the data) + Instantly or Smartlead (for sending). Total cost: ~$250/month. You get fresh funded startup leads with contacts, delivered weekly, and a tool to email them.

**If you need a general-purpose B2B database:**
Apollo's free tier is hard to beat. Upgrade when you need more credits.

**If you have budget and want everything:**
Clay + Sales Navigator + a sending tool. Build custom workflows that identify, research, and outreach in one pipeline.

**If you're enterprise:**
ZoomInfo or PitchBook, depending on whether you need contact data (ZoomInfo) or investment intelligence (PitchBook).

## The Bottom Line

Crunchbase is a good research tool, but it's not a sales tool. The funding data is delayed, there's no contact information, and you still need to build the outreach yourself.

The best alternative depends on what you're optimizing for: **freshness** (FundedList), **volume** (Apollo/ZoomInfo), **customization** (Clay), or **cost** (GrowthList).

For funded startup outreach specifically, the winning formula is: **fresh data + verified contacts + your own outreach stack.** Don't pay $20K/year for a platform when $250/month gets you the same result faster.
